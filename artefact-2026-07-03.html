OIRS: A Quick Reference Card for Stepping Away at the Right Moment

The four phases behind every session I run, and why the last one matters more than people think.

Most coaching advice focuses on what to say to a young player. This card focuses on the part that actually decides whether any of it sticks: knowing when to stop saying it.

Keep this nearby the next time you are watching a session or a match. Use it to notice which phase you are actually in, and whether you have been staying in it longer than the player needs you to.

1
O

Observe

Watch before you speak. Notice what the player is actually doing and try to work out why, before deciding whether anything needs to be said at all.

2
I

Intervene

Add one specific thing the player did not already have. A question works better than an answer. Keep it short enough that it does not become the new instruction they wait for.

3
R

Reinforce

Let the player try again and name what they got right when it lands. This confirms understanding without taking the decision back off them.

4
S

Step Away

Withdraw your voice once understanding has shown itself. This is the phase that proves whether the first three actually worked, and it is the one most adults skip.

Signs You Might Be Skipping Step Away

You are still talking during the fourth or fifth repetition of something the player already solved once on their own.

The player looks at you after every touch before deciding whether their choice was correct.

You feel uncomfortable staying quiet, even when the player is clearly working through the problem themselves.

You find yourself giving the same instruction in slightly different words across several sessions or matches.

The player performs well when you are close by and hesitates the moment you step back or go quiet.
