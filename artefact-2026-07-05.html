5 Questions To Ask After The Match

Questions that reinforce what went right, not just the scoreline or the mistakes.

This is a short companion to this week's blog post on Reinforce. Most post-match conversations focus on the score or the errors. These five questions do the opposite. They help you and your child notice, and name, the good decisions, regardless of the result.

1

"What's one decision you made today that you were pleased with?"

Not a goal, not a result. A decision. This puts the focus on judgement rather than outcome, which is the thing actually worth reinforcing.

2

"Was there a moment you tried something you don't normally try?"

This one rewards the attempt itself. A player who tries something new deserves recognition whether or not it came off.

3

"Did you notice a teammate do something well?"

Reinforcement doesn't have to come from you. Asking your child to spot good decisions in others builds the same habit of noticing.

4

"What would you keep doing next time, no matter the result?"

This separates the decision from the scoreline. A good decision stays good even on a day the result didn't go their way.

5

"What's something you noticed about your own game today?"

Open-ended, low pressure, and it hands the reflection back to the player rather than the parent doing it for them.
