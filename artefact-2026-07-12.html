Format Reference Card

The New Grassroots Football Formats, Explained

A quick reference for the FA's FutureFit changes landing this season, what's changing at each age group and what it's actually building in your child.

From this season, grassroots football looks different at every age group. Smaller teams, smaller pitches, further up the age range than most parents expect. It's not a smaller version of the game your child is missing out on. Here's what's changing, stage by stage, and the reason behind it.

U7

3v3

First Competitive Format

Three players a side means your child is never far from the action. At this age, involvement matters more than anything else, every touch is a small decision.

Builds: comfort on the ball, first touches under real pressure, willingness to try.
U8-U9

5v5

Small-Sided Football

A bit more space, a bit more structure, but still built around frequent touches and constant 1v1 and 2v2 moments rather than long spells off the ball.

Builds: 1v1 duels, quicker decisions, the start of reading space around them.
U10-U11

7v7

Small-Sided Football

More players, more shape, but still small enough that a hesitant player can't disappear into space for the whole match. Passing options open up alongside the individual moments.

Builds: scanning before the ball arrives, combining with teammates, decision speed under more options.
U12-U13

9v9

Bridging Format

The step up toward a full pitch, with enough space now to test decisions made under less pressure and more time, while still keeping numbers manageable.

Builds: positional awareness, reading the game over longer spells, tactical understanding.
U14+

11v11

Full-Sided Football

A full year later than it used to start. By this stage, players arrive with far more reps of the game's core problems already behind them, which is exactly why the bigger picture tends to make sense faster, not slower.

Builds: everything above, now applied to the full picture of the game.
Also Worth Knowing

Younger age groups no longer publish league tables. The focus stays on involvement and development rather than a Saturday morning result.

Deliberate heading is out at primary school ages, in line with the FA's heading guidance.

At U7 3v3, every player is on the pitch for the whole match, no substitutes sat out waiting for a turn.
