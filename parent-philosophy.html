My Parent Philosophy

Three principles. Written by you. For you. Before the season starts.

Every coach has a coaching philosophy. It shapes every decision they make under pressure. You are on that touchline every weekend, making decisions under pressure too. These three prompts help you choose your principles before the moment arrives, rather than reacting when it does.

01

Separate your journey from your child's

Your child's football experience belongs to them. What you hope for them and what they need from you are not always the same thing. This principle asks you to name the difference.

Write your principle

My role on the touchline is...

When things are not going my child's way, I will...

02

Be a student of the game, not a commentator

Watching only your child through a narrow lens leads to commentary, not support. Widening to the game itself changes what you see and what you say.

Write your principle

Instead of watching my child and judging every touch, I will focus on...

The one thing I will stop saying on the touchline is...

03

Create the right environment, not the outcome

You cannot control whether your child becomes a top player. You can control the environment around them. That includes the car journey, what happens after a bad game, and what you celebrate.

Write your principle

After a difficult game, the environment I want my child to come home to is...

The thing I want to celebrate more this season is...
